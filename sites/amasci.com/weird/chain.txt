I am a somewhat spiritual person myself.  I also practice brutal
self-honesty.  I say this because you may not like the following.  I'm not
trying to be superior, I'm just trying to point out an important negative
aspect of all this.  You can tell me "You're just a negative person!" 
"You're not spiritual!" I hear you.  Before I can fix my flaws, I must
first perceive them.  But before anyone lowers themselves to namecalling,
please read my thoughts below. 

Your message is redistributing wealth, yes.  ANd it really works.  But
this sort of thing operates by convincing a large number of people at the
bottom of a list to give money to a few people at the top of a list.  But
the message doesn't admit that this is what's happening.  Therefor the
whole thing is extremely dishonest.  In my opinion, participating in a
pyramid scheme is extremely "bad karma" because of the number of people
being lied to.  In comparison, lotteries and gambling are honest!  There,
lots of people lose money so that a few may win.  But at least everyone
knows this!  With the "pyramid scheme" letter below, the fact that most
participants lose money while THOSE WHO STARTED THE LETTER WIN BIG, is
hidden.  Was it done through ignorance?  Maybe.  People with good
intentions can harm others through ignorance.  But where $$$ is concerned,
we might also suspect greed.  Participants and instigators should either
be ashamed of their ignorant acts, or ashamed of their greed and
hypocrisy.  As spiritual people we must battle the illusory world which
draws us into narcissim.  We must conquer "self-importance", and try hard
to avoid letting our self-serving delusions suck us into acts which make
the world just a bit more of a rotten place to be.  Negative actions, even
if obscured by denial and intentional self-blindness, are still negative
actions. 

When a "pyramid" letter is first launched, the person at the top of the
list gets money.  More and more people receive the message, and the people
farther down the list get less money.  Finally the message spreads to a
major part of the world, and people start ignoring the circulating
message.  When all is said and done, a very large number of people have
sent money up to those near the top of the pyramid, yet since there are no
people below them, the huge multitude at the end of the process don't get
money in return. 

Think about it.  THe message does not create money, it just convinces
many people to send money to few people.  The instigator of the message
benefits the most.  Even if the message reached everyone on earth, all
that would happen is that we would each give $33, and each receive $33.
But when thousands of people send the message back to Amber and those on
the message list, will they part with their large earnings and
redistribute it again?  You think they will?  Maybe.  But then, we aren't
supposed to send the message to people on the list!

The message says that it's not a chain-letter.  Was this statement made in
ignorance of what a "chain letter" is?  Of course that message is a
chain-letter.  It is a typical pyramid-scheme chain letter, originally
known as "ponzi scheme" long ago.  Pyramid schemes are tempting because
they really work.  Robbery really works too.  What is worse; to break into
one person's home, or to take unearned money from thousands of people,
while covering up the fact that they won't get money back in return?

If you honestly want to redistribute wealth, go buy lottery tickets, or
anonymously give money to a needy family.  If you want to receive because
you have given, then go volunteer at your favorite charity.  But never
send money to a pyramid-scheme participant on the internet.  As
reprehensible acts go, it's a far worse act than gambling, because you
become a sort of "casino owner" rather than a fellow gambler.  Your
chances for making money are far higher than those to whom you send the
chain letter.

I think we should all send our best wishes to Amber and those on her list. 
They have caused a slight amount of harm to a very large number of people. 
They have aquired soiled earnings, participated in lying to friends, and
have spread a type of "email disease" across the internet.  When they
eventually come to confront their actions, I think they'll need all the
kind thoughts they can get. 




Think twice about it all.  Be self-honest, don't let temptation and denial
cloud your vision.  Send back the money, you did not earn it properly, and
it has the curse of the Pyramid Scheme attached to it.
