I have a system set up where they can jump into a confined space as situated between a partially raised glass storm door, and propped main door. I say "propped" because I use a bungee cord to keep the main door nearly closed, while still allowing the cats to push their way into the house when they want in. This system works really well, keeping things like flies, wasps, and mosquitoes out. Having said that...

It was during a pleasant evening on a Sunday at around 8:35 pm on 09JUNE2013, where I was watching TV. I had just checked the time because I was browsing for something else to watch, when I heard one of my cats cry out in a fit. I at first thought it was Bo, because she oftentimes cries like that when she'd engaged with another cat or animal that has bested her. My first thought was that a car or something hit her.

At that same moment, as I began to open the glass storm door come to her aid, she flipped over and began to "engage" with something unseen to my eyes. She curled up on her backside in order to bring whatever it was closer so that she could attack it. And she did. They rolled out into the middle of the porch deck, where she got whatever it was that had her, to let her go. Immediately she bolted through the now open door and into the house. And whatever had her, I watched as it bolted through the brush growing up around the deck area. I know something was there, even though I could not see it, because of the way the brush exploded apart at it raced through.

I was just standing there, awestruck, not believing what I had just seen (or didn't see, take your pick.) I then turned my attention to my cat to check her out. She was okay, just rattled. I went back to the deck where this, this... I don't know what to call it, really, but right in the same spot where my cat had fought whatever it was, there began this slow manifestation of a wet-looking spiral that trailed out to the same place where whatever it was, bolted through the bushes. And there it was, this weird looking spiral of what looked like water, sitting in the middle of an otherwise dry deck with absolutely no weather in the area.

So I got my digital camera, and took pictures of it. I was only able to salvage one really good one because I was amped up and just too shaky with the others. But at least I got one! And I touched the stuff, too. I looked like water, it felt like water, and it smelled like water. Not knowing what it was, I didn't press my luck any further.

And here is where things get even weirder, if that's possible. Having no understanding of what I just witnessed, I parked the memory of it in the back of my head. Then, on 19JUNE2013, exactly ten days later, I'm watching "Paranormal Witness" on the SciFi Channel. The episode is called "The Lost Boy." As the story goes; and in an effortcommunicate with the living; the spirit of the dead child made a weird looking spiral out of material in the middle of the kitchen floor. All I know is, you normally don't get answers to mysteries like this in any given lifetime. But it seems I was just handed one. As weird and strange as it all sounds, or is -- it is what happened -- and I should know because it happened to me. Otherwise, I would have never believed it. I just wish cats could talk.

This is not a report of unusual phenomena, but I do have something helpful to contribute!
In the "odd animals" section I've read I think four different posts regarding hummingbird-like creatures, one described it as like a little furry lobster with wings, all mention the super-fast wings and hovering and the butterfly-like proboscis. Well, I have seen these guys many times! They are called hummingbird moths, or hawk moths. Scientific name is Hemaris thysbe. If you google it you can find lots of pictures and info on them. I can certainly see why people are baffled by these guys, when I first saw one I couldn't figure out what the hell it was either. They mimic a real hummingbird so well, but are also so clearly insects, it's freaky! I see them every summer for the last several years in the flowerbeds, here in southwest Wisconsin. I've always wanted to catch one so that I can get a really good look at it, study it, but no way- they are far too fast! And also I wouldn't want to hurt them, they seem very delicate and rare. He has always had a German Shepherd dog to guard the house and yard and we (grandchildren) would all take turns taking care of her (give water, food, brushing, etc.). One day, I was brushing her coat when I noticed a large tick that was attached to her. Without thinking, I pulled it off of her and was about to smash it when I noticed the coloration of the tick. It was the colors of the stars and stripes except different as the white stars were on a red field and there were blue stripes. I know that someone would not take the time to color a tick the stars and stripes so I was quite perplexed at this even for my young little mind. I put it down and watched it walk around to make sure that it was real and sure enough it started to walk away. The colors were really bright against the body of the tick as it looked just like a balloon where as when the balloon is small the colors are bright but as the air expands inside the colors tend to fade and spread but the colors were bright in this case. The colors didn't look painted on also, it looked more like it was printed on the tick just like a balloon. I would estimate the body to be about a half inch from mandibles to the end of the body. I didn't notice the legs if they were different colors or just brown so I take it that it was just brown. At that time, I wanted to call someone but no one was around and I was afraid to leave the tick outside as it would crawl away and I couldn't bring it in as I had gotten in trouble for bringing in other creepy crawlers (frogs, millipedes, you name it...). I decided to just run as fast as I can and get my brother who was inside watching TV and show him. I only took what I felt was about seconds to get him but that was all it took for the tick to be gone. We looked everywhere for it but it was nowhere in sight. I know this is difficult to prove but I know what I saw and it was burned into my memory.
